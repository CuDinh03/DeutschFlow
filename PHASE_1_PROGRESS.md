# DeutschFlow 12-Week B1 Plan

## Executive Summary

Revised learning plan optimized for faster progression with equivalent outcomes:

- **Old duration:** 14 weeks
- **New duration:** 12 weeks
- **Expected gain:** ~26% faster with comparable effectiveness
- **Sequence:** phased learning pipeline instead of strict linear sequencing
- **Key shift:** AI Speaking and SRS start from day 1

The plan is organized around three phases that move from input to production to integration, while keeping vocabulary, grammar, speaking, and spaced repetition interleaved from the beginning.

## Learning Science Principles

1. **Comprehensible Input**  
   Learners should understand roughly 80-90% of what they see and hear from the start.

2. **Spaced Repetition (SM-2)**  
   Built-in SRS with review intervals of 1, 3, 7, 14, and 30 days to target long-term retention.

3. **Interleaving**  
   Vocabulary, grammar, and speaking are mixed from week 1 instead of being isolated into separate blocks.

4. **Retrieval Practice**  
   Production tasks like speaking and writing reinforce the same language material that was introduced through input.

5. **Contextual Grammar**  
   Grammar is learned through patterns in context rather than explicit rules-first instruction.

6. **Early Speaking**  
   AI dialogue practice begins on day 1, not week 7.

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1-4, 32 hours)

**Goal:** establish core input, confidence, and survival speaking.

- **Vocabulary:** A1 core, 200-300 words
- **Grammar:** articles and present tense through input, not rule memorization
- **Speaking:** greetings and introductions
- **AI Speaking:** starts from day 1 with simple dialogues
- **SRS:** active from day 1 with built-in review scheduling

**Outcomes:**
- Recognize and understand high-frequency A1 words in context
- Handle short self-introductions and basic greeting exchanges
- Build a stable review habit early

### Phase 2 — Production (Weeks 5-8, 36 hours)

**Goal:** turn input into controlled output.

- **Vocabulary:** expand to 500 words total, adding ~200 A2 words
- **Grammar:** past tense and noun cases through speaking and guided tasks
- **Writing:** sentence completion progressing to short paragraphs
- **Interview prep:** begins in this phase

**Outcomes:**
- Produce more varied sentences under light pressure
- Start combining grammar patterns across contexts
- Connect vocabulary recall with actual production

### Phase 3 — Fluency (Weeks 9-12, 38 hours)

**Goal:** consolidate toward B1 performance.

- **Vocabulary:** expand to 700 words total, adding ~200 more complex B1 words
- **Interview mode:** full 5-phase interview flow
- **Mock test:** CEFR B1 practice and readiness check
- **Graduation:** B1 level confirmed

**Outcomes:**
- Sustain a 25-30 minute conversation
- Maintain accuracy across major tenses and cases
- Demonstrate confidence even when making mistakes

## Time Investment

- **Total:** 106 hours
- **Average daily load:** 3+ hours/day
- **Efficiency target:** ~3x more efficient than traditional classroom pacing

### Comparison
- **DeutschFlow:** 106 hours
- **Classroom:** 200+ hours
- **Intensive school:** 240 hours

## Exit Criteria — End of Week 12

The learner is considered B1-ready when all of the following are achieved:

- **Vocabulary:** 700 words with 90%+ retention
- **Speaking:** 25-30 minute conversation at B1 fluency level
- **Grammar:** major tenses and cases at 85%+ accuracy
- **Confidence:** comfortable producing language while making mistakes
- **CEFR level:** B1 confirmed

## Phase 1 Implementation Alignment

Phase 1 should be treated as the foundation slice of this 12-week plan, not as a standalone linear module. The system should therefore support:

- AI Speaking from day 1
- SRS from day 1
- vocabulary intake and review loops from day 1
- grammar as contextual patterns inside the input/production flow
- early speaking even at the simplest dialogue level
